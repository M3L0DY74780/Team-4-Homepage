What are cyberattacks?

A cyberattack is when someone tries to access, steal, or damage data or systems without permission.

The amount of common cyberattacks occured throughtout the years:

Year Number
2022 5.4 billion
2023 731 million
2024 5.6 billion
2025 (progress) 1.9 million per week

These cyberattacks consist of:

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages that try to steal personal information.
  • Malware: Harmful software like viruses or trojans.
  • Ransomware: A type of malware that locks files and asks for money to unlock them.
  • DDoS: Attacks that overload a website so it stops working.
  • Man-in-the-Middle: Someone secretly reads or changes messages between two people.
  • SQL Injection: An attack that tricks a website into showing private information.
  • Zero-Day Exploit: An attack that uses a bug in software before it's fixed.

How to Protect Yourself from Cyberattacks

Strong Passwords
Use complex and unique passwords for each of your accounts.
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Enable 2FA to add an extra layer of security.
Update Software
Regularly install updates for your operating system and antivirus software.
Be Cautious with Links
Don't click on suspicious links in emails or messages.
Backup Important Data
Store copies of essential files in secure cloud or offline storage.
