Introduction to why protecting personal data important

In recent years, almost everything we do is connect to the internet. Whether it's chatting with friends, online shopping or keeping up with socials. However, do you ever notice that you are sharing a lot of personal information online? Once this information gets leaked, it can cause serious problems like scams, stolen identity, or hackers/ scammers having access to your bank details. It's important to keep yourself safe, protect your personal data, and making sure you are in control of what you share.


Why you need protect your personal data

  1. Prevent identity theft:
    2. Hackers and scammers can steal your NRIC/ passport numbers to impersonate you, committing crimes in your name.

  2. Avoid financial loss:
    3. Once you get hacked, they are able to steal your credit card and bank information. Allowing them to make purchases or transfer your money to their own bank account.

  3. Safeguard family and friends:
    4. When scammers have access to your contacts, they are able to send fake messages and links to your family and friends that look real so that they click on it and all their information will go to the scammer. They are also able to create deepfake videos and imitate your voice to convince your loved ones that it is actually you.

How to protect your personal data online

  • Keep your Software up to date:
    • Hackers often look for software that has not been updated which has weak points that are not patched. So, whenever there is a new Software update, make sure you install them as soon as possible so that it reduces the chances of hackers being able to hack into your devices.

  • Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication:
    • Having an easy password defeats the purpose of having a password as hackers are able to guess it easily. Using strong passwords and two-factor authentication can help reinforce the security of your devices.

  • Use secure websites:
    • Always check if webistes has "https://" before entering personal information.

  • Avoid using public Wi-Fi:
    • If you really have to use public Wi-Fi networks, make sure that you do not login to your bank accounts, online shopping or logging into any account that will expose your personal informations. Also, use a VPN so that hackers will not be able to hack you easliy.

  • Beware of suspicious links
    • When you recieve an email, SMS or messages from an unknown number, make sure to not click onto any links. As it will allow hackers to steal information right in front of your eyes and you are not able to do anything about it.
