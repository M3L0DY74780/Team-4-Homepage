Recongizing Online scams

Recongizing online scams can help you know what type of scams you are facing and what to do from there.

Signs of a scam:

  • "Too good to be true" discounts or offers
  • An unknown person asks for your help and wants money or your personal information
  • Receive a message stating that there is suspicious activity on you device
  • Wanting you to click on a suspicious link to make a payment

    • Avoiding Online Scams

    Using these online scams as an example, let me show you how to avoid them!
    Type of Scam Solution
    "Too good to be true" discounts or offers Visit the official website or social media account of the company offering the discount. Tis can help you find out if the discount is trustable or it is a scam.
    An unknown person asks for your help and wants money or your personal information Be cautious with money appeals and only give what you're willing to lose if the story can’t be verified.
    Receive a message stating that there is suspicious activity on you device Check where the message originated from. If it is from the phone company you purchased from, you will be able to further contact them. If not, it could be a scam.
    Wanting you to click on a suspicious link to make a payment Do not click the link. Clicking the link can cause your phone to get a virus which the hacker can steal your personal information.
